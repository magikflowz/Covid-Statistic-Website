What are our Responsibilities now a days

When you’re young and healthy, your risk of developing severe illness is lower. But young people play an important role in stopping the spread of the COVID-19 coronavirus and keeping those who are most vulnerable from getting sick. It’s everyone’s responsibility.

Even though the danger of COVID-19 may be greater for older generations, young people are just as likely to become infected as any other age group. Whether they get sick or remain asymptomatic, they are spreading the virus. There are things they can do to slow the spread to protect hospitals from being overwhelmed and to make a difference in their community.

Tips for people who teach and volunteer with youth



  • > Use the pandemic to help teach young people to be good citizens. Create ways they can contribute and make a difference for others.

  • > Brain science reminds us that adolescents’ frontal lobes are still developing, which means that skills like impulse control, delayed gratification, and realizing the consequences of actions are not fully in place.

  • > Some of these practices have been politicized. Stick to facts and trusted information. Set a good example by modeling these practices yourself.
